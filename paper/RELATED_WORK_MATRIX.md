# CIRCE Related Work Matrix

This matrix is a reviewer-facing positioning aid, not a finished bibliography.
Where exact local paper metadata is unavailable, the row uses a representative
family and marks the citation gap explicitly so later literature work can fill
the precise citation and venue.

| Thread | Representative work/family | Venue or evidence type | Key claim | Missing piece | CIRCE reviewer-facing gap | Product moat implication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| LLM persona simulation | Prompt-only synthetic users; persona-conditioned survey respondents (citation gap: exact local bibliography unavailable) | Empirical demos, survey simulation studies, and product prototypes | Personas can generate diverse qualitative responses for imagined users. | Lacks closed-loop joint calibration against both micro-choice fit and macro-emergence distortion. | CIRCE treats persona behavior as a calibrated simulator output rather than as plausible text alone. | Product can say the report is not merely persona narration; it is tied to calibrated evidence fields. |
| Generative agent social worlds | Memory/reflection/planning agent families for social interaction (citation gap: exact local bibliography unavailable) | Agent simulation papers and demos | Language agents can produce coherent social episodes and interaction traces. | Lacks prompt/model/mode/artifact-level manifest that separates dry-run plumbing from local/live evidence. | CIRCE adds evidence manifests and mode-aware claim boundaries to interaction runs. | Customer-facing evidence cards expose model, mode, command, metrics, artifacts, and boundary language. |
| Agent-based modeling | Voter, contagion, threshold, and opinion-dynamics models | Formal models, simulation benchmarks, and controlled synthetic data | Macro patterns can be studied under explicit transition rules. | Does not by itself adapt LLM prompt or model behavior through closed-loop joint calibration. | CIRCE uses ABM as controlled emergence reference while evaluating LLM-driven interaction policies. | Product can frame ABM lanes as controlled stress tests rather than ungrounded scenario stories. |
| Discrete choice calibration | MNL, nested logit, mixed logit, and transport-choice calibration | Econometric and transportation modeling evidence | Choice probabilities can be identified and calibrated under declared assumptions. | Lacks simultaneous macro-emergence distortion reporting for multi-agent social dynamics. | CIRCE keeps micro-choice fit visible while adding emergence metrics and manifest-level evidence gates. | Product can explain individual preference fit and group-level sensitivity in the same report. |
| Probabilistic predictor calibration | Temperature scaling, isotonic calibration, Platt-style post-hoc calibration | ML calibration benchmarks and reliability diagrams | Predictive probabilities can be adjusted to improve calibration metrics on held-out data. | Does not define a social-simulation loop with macro trajectory distortion and claim audit fields. | CIRCE separates probability calibration from social emergence calibration and records both. | Product avoids selling a probability-calibrated model as a complete market simulator. |
| Prompt optimization | TextGrad-style, ProTeGi-style, DSPy-style, and prompt-search families (citation gap: exact local bibliography unavailable) | Optimization papers, framework docs, and task benchmarks | Prompt updates can improve task metrics through feedback or search. | Lacks manifest-backed social-science claim boundaries and closed-loop joint calibration across micro and macro metrics. | CIRCE makes prompt updates acceptable only when lane metrics and evidence manifests support the claim. | Product can show why a prompt edit was accepted, rejected, or marked evidence-limited. |
| LLM-as-survey or silicone-sample studies | LLM respondents, synthetic panels, and demographic persona steering (citation gap: exact local bibliography unavailable) | Survey-method studies and benchmark comparisons | LLM populations may approximate selected survey distributions under some prompts. | Lacks artifact-level distinction between qualitative plausibility, dry-run plumbing, and local/live model evidence. | CIRCE requires each distributional claim to map to a manifest class and benchmark lane. | Product can use sensitivity language instead of presenting synthetic respondents as real respondents. |
| Computational social science simulation | Synthetic populations, policy experiments, and scenario-level social analysis | CSS simulation papers, policy notebooks, and reproducibility packages | Simulations can support exploratory analysis of social processes and policy scenarios. | Lacks prompt/model/mode/artifact-level manifest in many notebook-style workflows. | CIRCE treats auditability and claim boundaries as part of the method rather than as supplementary logging. | Product gets a defensible report pipeline with visible evidence gates and blocked-claim reasons. |
| Benchmark and leaderboard reporting | Multi-domain social or agent benchmarks (citation gap: exact local bibliography unavailable) | Benchmark suites, leaderboards, and reproduction artifacts | Standardized lanes make methods comparable across tasks. | May not distinguish causal-only, emergence-only, and joint calibration effects. | CIRCE requires discrimination experiments that isolate causal calibration, emergence calibration, and joint calibration. | Product proof points can cite which lane and ablation support a claim. |
| Audit and provenance tooling | Experiment trackers, model cards, data cards, and reproducibility manifests | Engineering audit artifacts and governance documentation | Runs, data, and models can be documented for review. | Usually documents evidence after the run rather than serving as an executable claim gate. | CIRCE turns manifest fields into a claim predicate for manuscript and product wording. | Product can block unsupported report claims before customer delivery. |
